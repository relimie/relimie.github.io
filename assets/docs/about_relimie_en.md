## Mindful Enjoyment, Not Sacrifice

Relimie isn't just about quitting drinking, and it's more than an alcohol tracker: it's a mindful drinking companion for those who enjoy a drink but want to prevent their habits from becoming an addiction — without the guilt or judgment that often comes with tracking.

In Relimie, everything revolves around your **[Personal Baseline](faq.html#what-is-a-personal-baseline)** (which is why we call our method "baseline-drinking"). Instead of a rigid daily limit that feels like a failure if you miss it, your baseline is a long-term anchor. Life happens: some nights you'll have an extra glass, others you won't drink at all. What matters is the trend. Over time, your habits naturally gravitate back toward your goal.

Tapping your glowing **Orb** shows you where you stand at a glance: **Green** — you're comfortably within your baseline; **Gold** — you're slightly over your limit; **Red** — you should consider pausing for a few days to bring your average back in line. The green signs also show you how many drinks you have avoided so far. You can use this information to adjust your baseline down and **[reduce your consumption step by step](faq.html#how-does-the-baseline-help-me-reduce-my-drinking)**, if you wish.

To help you **[choose your baseline](faq.html#how-do-i-choose-the-right-baseline)**, we provide data from WHO recommendations and average values for your country and gender. These serve as orientation until you find your personal values. Relimie also supports a variety of measurement units, so you can use those you understand best (like grams of pure alcohol or standard US units).

Keep in mind that while baseline-drinking is a powerful helper for controlling your behavior, it won't do the work for you. You need to put in the effort to stick to your baseline and log accurately. Since the baseline is based on aggregation, it needs some time to "kick in" as it collects data. Don't be discouraged if you don't see it change immediately, even though it becomes available right after onboarding.

Curious where baseline-drinking came from? It grew out of a single number and one person's struggle to stay in control — [read the story behind Relimie](story.html).
